The Problem

Students

South Africa currently experiences chronic levels of youth unemployment. A contributor to this is the mismatch between skills required in the labour market and those provided by education systems. Graduates lack industry experience.

Businesses

Small businesses can't hire management consultants. Undergraduates, however, have skills that could be beneficial to businesses.

A diverse range of business analysts, software developers, marketers, engineers, financial consultants, creatives, animators and graphic designers exist to help businesses solve their problems.

The Solution

A software platform where students can find jobs based on their skills and interests. The platform allows businesses to post problems that need solving or jobs that need to be done. Vendi's algorithm will provide a list of students that best suites the business's criteria and budget.

How It Works

Students first sign up with Vendi, and enter their details (such as their location, university, degree and work experience) along with skills and interests. Vendi then uses this data to provide businesses with potential students that could solve their problems.

Step One

Businesses post details about the job that needs to be performed and click "Find Me a Student". Details include a description of the job, the location, the date and time, the number of students wanted and a preference to pick the selection pool based on price or experience.

Step Two

The business is then presented with a selection of students that best match the details and skills required by the job. Each student has a rating (based on past work) and an experience level.
